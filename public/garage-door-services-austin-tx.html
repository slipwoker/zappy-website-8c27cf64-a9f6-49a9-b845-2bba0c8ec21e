Our Workmanship Standards

What Every Irongate Job Includes — Without Exception

Every garage door repair and installation we perform in Austin follows a strict set of professional standards. No shortcuts, no guesswork, no mess left behind. Here's exactly what you can expect when an Irongate technician shows up at your door.

Full Safety Inspection & Door Balance Test

Before we touch a single component, every visit starts with a thorough safety check. We test the door's balance by disconnecting the opener and lifting the door manually to the midpoint — a properly balanced door holds position without assistance. We inspect all moving parts for wear, check the auto-reverse sensors, verify travel limits, and confirm the emergency release is functioning. This isn't a formality. An unbalanced or unsafe door puts your family and your opener motor at risk, and we catch those problems before they become emergencies.

  • Manual balance test at every visit
  • Auto-reverse & sensor verification
  • Full hardware safety audit before work begins

Photo-Documented Recommendations — No Pressure, Full Transparency

If we find additional wear or components that are approaching end-of-life, we photograph them and show you directly on our phone. You'll see exactly what we see — a cracked cable, a worn roller, a spring with visible coil separation — before we ever recommend a repair. We explain what it means, what happens if it fails, and what it costs to fix. Then the decision is entirely yours. We never manufacture urgency or recommend work that isn't warranted. This photo-documented approach is how we've built a reputation for honest garage door repair across Austin TX and every neighborhood we serve.

  • Real photos of every flagged component
  • Plain-language explanation of each finding
  • Zero-pressure recommendations — you decide

When springs need replacing, we offer standard and high-cycle options — the latter rated for 25,000 to 50,000 cycles instead of the builder-grade 10,000 — so you're not back in the same situation two years from now. Every part we install comes with a clearly stated warranty, explained in plain English before we begin: what it covers, how long it lasts, and exactly how to reach us if something isn't right. When the job is done, we collect every old spring, broken roller, and scrap piece of hardware. Your garage floor will be cleaner when we leave than it was when we arrived. That's not a bonus — it's the baseline.
